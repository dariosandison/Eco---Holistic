If your sleep feels “off”, the simplest lever is often **light timing** — especially morning daylight.

This isn’t a hack. It’s a small routine that helps your body clock know when “day” starts.

> **Quick links:** [Sleep topic →](/topics/sleep) • [Natural sleep support shortlist →](/best-natural-sleep-support)

## At a glance
- **Do this first:** daylight soon after waking.
- **Start small:** 5–10 minutes outdoors (even on cloudy days).
- **Consistency beats intensity:** do it most days.

## The 10-minute plan
### Step 1 — pick a realistic time window
Within **60 minutes of waking** is a good target. If that’s hard, do it after your first drink/coffee or school run.

### Step 2 — go outside (don’t overthink it)
- Stand on the doorstep, walk around the block, or take a short dog walk.
- Cloudy UK light still counts.

### Step 3 — pair it with something you already do
Examples:
- Morning coffee outside
- First phone call outside
- Quick bin run + 2 minutes of daylight

### Step 4 — make evenings easier (optional)
If you can, dim lights slightly in the last hour before bed and keep the bedroom dark.

## If you work nights or shift patterns
You can still use this idea — but timing is different. The goal is to anchor your “day start” consistently relative to your schedule.

## What to expect
- Many people notice an easier wind‑down after **7–14 days** of doing it most mornings.
- It won’t fix everything, but it’s a strong base layer.

<Note>
If you have persistent insomnia, severe snoring, breathing pauses, or daytime sleepiness, seek medical advice. This is educational guidance, not a diagnosis.
</Note>
