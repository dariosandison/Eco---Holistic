Hard water is normal in many parts of the UK. For most people it’s mainly a **comfort + practicality** issue (limescale, soap not lathering), not something to panic about.

> **Quick links:** [Water →](/topics/water) • [Skin health →](/topics/skin-health) • [Shower filters →](/best-shower-filters-uk-hard-water)

## At a glance
- **What hard water does:** causes limescale and can make skin/hair feel “drier” for some people.
- **What it usually doesn’t mean:** that your tap water is “unsafe”.
- **Best first step:** adjust routines (temperature, duration, moisturise) before buying anything.

## What you might notice at home
- White chalky residue in kettles, taps, shower screens
- Soap/shampoo feels like it doesn’t rinse cleanly
- Skin feels tighter after hot showers (especially in winter)
- Towels/bed linen feel “crispy” if detergent is overdosed

## Common myths (the calm version)
### Myth 1: “Hard water is toxic”
UK drinking water is regulated. People filter for **taste/odour** or preference — not because they “must” for safety.

### Myth 2: “A shower filter will fix eczema”
Some people feel a comfort improvement, but skin issues have multiple drivers. Treat a shower filter as a **comfort experiment**, not a cure.

### Myth 3: “More product fixes the problem”
In hard water areas, more shampoo/detergent often leaves more residue — which can feel worse.

## Comfort-first routine tweaks (no spend)
- **Shorter, cooler showers** (hot water can be drying for many people)
- **Use less cleanser** and rinse longer
- **Moisturise while skin is still slightly damp**
- **Laundry:** use the minimum detergent dose that works; add an extra rinse for bedding if you’re reactive

## When a product can help
### If you’re buying a shower filter
Choose based on:
- Cartridge availability in the UK
- Ease of replacement (you will need to replace it)
- Clear expectations: comfort-first
